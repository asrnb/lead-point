# Dental Emergencies

If you have severe, uncontrolled bleeding, facial swelling that's affecting
your breathing or swallowing, or facial trauma from an accident, go to the
nearest emergency room immediately — this is beyond what any dental office
can treat.

## During office hours
Call us and describe what's happening. We hold same-day emergency slots every
day we're open for: severe toothache, a knocked-out or broken tooth, a lost
filling or crown causing pain, or an abscess/swelling that isn't
breathing-related.

## After hours
Our after-hours line has a recorded message with next steps; it is not
monitored live overnight. For anything that can wait until morning (mild
sensitivity, a lost filling with no pain, a chipped tooth with no sharp
edges), leave a message and we'll call back when we open. For anything more
urgent than that, please go to an urgent care clinic or emergency room.

## Knocked-out tooth — first aid
1. Pick it up by the crown (top), not the root.
2. Rinse gently with water if dirty — don't scrub it.
3. Try to place it back in the socket if possible; if not, keep it in milk or
   your own saliva.
4. Get to us or an ER within 30 minutes for the best chance of saving the
   tooth.
